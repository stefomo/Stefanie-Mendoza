LGBT Community Network


Scope:
Web Design (Figma & WordPress)

The Team:
Aivy P., Mari W., & Lara C.

My Role:
Product Manager

Organization:
LGBT Community Network

The LGBT Community Network is a nonprofit that works to support lesbian, gay, bisexual, & transgender communities all across the state of California.

Summary


The original LGBT Community website had a poor navigation system which led to low online engagement and ineffective communication between community members and resources, necessitating a redesign.


This project entailed a comprehensive overhaul of the main umbrella website and the local subsidiaries’ sites. We aimed to create a responsive platform where visitors can effortlessly explore and engage with each other and the LGBT Community resources.






The revamped website retains the essence of the previous platform but enriches it with additional information and interactions.

We integrated new features such as a dedicated leadership page, an events calendar, and a donation portal, resulting in increased engagement and participation within the LGBT Community Network, much to our client's satisfaction!






Starting Point


We commenced our redesign by conducting a heuristic evaluation of the site to identify potential challenges and design opportunities. Additionally, we communicated with our client to understand their specific requests and requirements for the final product.


We condensed our findings into key points that our redesign must address.


Challenges concerning the client’s requests:

    -> How can we unite the organization’s vision with the website’s design?
    -> How do we design the umbrella and local subsidiaries for better accessibility and smoother onboarding for volunteers and users?

Fundamental problems we identified during our evaluation:

  • Challenging navigation experience
  • Confusing website functionality and user journey
  • Difficulty and ambiguity in understanding website content and purpose


Research


To kickstart our research process, I formulated a roadmap and chose essential artifacts to aid us in conducting thorough research. We created a comprehensive CMS Comparison chart aimed at idenitfying the most suitable management system tailored for our client's needs. This also required us to conduct a Content Inventory Audit to assess the current website's content landscape to help us pinpoint areas of improvement. Subsequently, we crafted a user flow map to refine and optimize navigation paths to ensure an improved browsing experience across the site.


CMS Chart

The existing website management system had its limitations and was not suitable for simultaneously managing multiple sites. It was also crucial for the client to be able to learn how to manage the website independently with ease so finding the most suitable platform was essential. The CMS (Web Content Management) Comparison chart we provided to our client had a detailed overview of various CMS options that would suit their needs to facilitate an informed decision-making process.


Fig. 2. Narrowed down version of our CMS Comparison Chart


Due to Wordpress's capabilities to run a multisite network, integrate with third party tools easily (SEO tools), and offer a directory of plugins, the team and the client began the transfer from Joomla to Wordpress.


Inventory Audit

To identify gaps within the website, our team conducted a Content Inventory Audit to assess the existing content to identify redundant and outdated information that needed to be updated or removed. This informed decisions on necessary changes.


Fig. 3. Preview of our Content Inventory



User Flow

Following the audit, we developed a new user flow map to illustrate the changes we wanted in the website's navigation, while also showcasing how pages are prioritized based on user and client needs. This allowed us to effectively map out features from both the main website and the individual subsidiary sites, ensuring consistency across the entire platform.




Fig.11 Site Map




User Research


It was also essential for us to interview and survey volunteers to understand their pain-points and user journeys with the current site to determine how a redesign could best address these issues. The team conducted user interviews and crafted a survey tailored for volunteers and first-time users, aiming to gather insights into their experiences with both the network and the website.


Fig. 4. Synthesized user interview board



Fig. 5. Preview of Survey Results




We interviewed with five LGBT Community network volunteers and collected feedback through a survey, which received over thirty responses from LGBTQ+ individuals to gathered perspectives about the website and help us identify areas for improvement


Synthesizing insights from our user research, we found 3 main takeaways.

1. The website is not engaging and feels more like a newsletter

2. Website information needs to be more consistently updated

3. Resources should be better organized and there should be an emphasis on local resources

Completing the first Redesign




Guided by our research, the team created several sketches for each subsection on the umbrella site, each one aimed at reorganizing the layout and content.

Our design emphasized a user-friendly navigation flow and functionality to fulfill the organization's goal of updating visitors with event information & community insights.

After receiving feedback from the client, we began prototyping, implementing, and refining each web page. This prototype’s goal was to make it easier for the client to consistently update website information, coupled with improving user access to community resources, all the while ensuring that users remain engaged with the website experience.

Testing and Iterations



Concluding the development of our first implemented version, The team and I wanted to continue building on the current design of the website by conducting thorough testing. This testing aimed to ensure that our work was in line with the LGBT Community's objectives and the user needs we identified through interviews.


Additionally, we needed to distinguish differences between the umbrella site and the local subsidiaries in order to effectively design for each.


Insights regarding the differences between the umbrella and subsidiaries





Usability Testing



The team and I carried out usability testing on the updated platform with first-time users who identified as LGBTQ+. First-time users were chosen rather than experienced volunteers due to their lack of knowledge of the previous site.

Our goal was to determine whether or not the redesigned site would truly be more effective and user-friendly for anyone, regardless of their experience with the LGBT Community Network.



Fig.12 Usability testing documentation




Based on our findings and user feedback, we needed to focus on


Emphasizing the organization's goal and intentions

Reorganizing the flow of information on the website to encourage users to learn about events, volunteer, and donate

    Improving accessibility and consistency of the text and navigation of the website
  • -> Users should be able to quickly navigate between the organization's information, services, and resources



Updated Prototypes



Following our findings from our usability testing and client feedback, we began redeveloping our initial designs. This was aimed at bringing more intention to the umbrella site purpose vs. the local chapter sites, while also prioritizing and highlighting features such as donations and resources. Our objective was to ensure a clearer understanding and better engagement with our redesign through evident call to actions and restructuring the flow of information on each web page.

This involved crafting new sketches which were refined into high-fidelity mockups, that would serve as the groundwork for implementation & deployment.

Conclusion



This project included two iterative phases of research and design. My team and I initiated our research with a heuristic evaluation and then began developing low-fidelity sketches and prototypes, culminating in the development of the final user interface.

Insights into my design process and objectives:


    🗓 Provided bi-weekly deliverables, clarifications, and ideas to the client to receive feedback.
  • Completed design sprints by the scheduled deadlines throughout the project timeline

    👥 Together with the designers, I devised a comprehensive testing plan and guide, facilitating user interviews and usability testing to gather ongoing feedback from users and the client.
  • Performed user research by interviewing current LGBT Community volunteers and a broad set of first-time users to provide us with valuable insights into their perspectives and experiences with the website
  • In the design sprints, I led, my team and I to conduct usability testing to evaluate the website's current state and our design progress.

    💭 We concentrated our efforts on redesigning the website with a clear focus on enhancing navigation, particularly with the anticipation of adding more components in the future.
  • This involved implementing intuitive navigation structures, such as streamlined menus, hierarchical organization, and user-friendly search functionality. By prioritizing these elements, we aimed to ensure that users can effortlessly find their way around the site, even as it evolves with additional content and features.
  • Using design tools such as the Miro boards, prototype personas, iterative model, and a functioning Figma demo was crucial to implementing an effective redesign.

Impact & Next Steps



During our extensive testing of the prototypes, my team and I observed a significant improvement in users' understanding of the LGBT community’s mission. The final round of usability testing also showed that users found more opportunities to engage with the LGBT Community's events and social media on the redesigned website. This was essential to confirm that our well-researched design choices were effective for users. In our next steps, I would like to implement our features into the WordPress website more effectively and intend to incorporate more imagery from the LGBT community's volunteer events to create a more inclusive and wholesome presence.
